I've been chronically online on tech twitter these past few months, and here's my predictions for the future:

1/ In a large influx of ai generated content, more people would look for content by humans.

This would involve:

a) human only content behind a paywall, to keep out bots and such
b) a badge to verify that your account is human by means of an id.

2/ Community > Product

Anyone can build software.

It's incredibly easy for you to create the next CapCut.
It's incredibly difficult to create the next Twitter.

The only way to future-proof your startup is if it involves community

3/ Automations will take over the world.

Expect lots of layoffs moving forward.
If your job involves a repetitive task that doesn't involve manual labor, pivot quickly.

Find a skill ai can't do, and double down.

4/ Majority of people outside tech twitter have no idea what's coming

These is the best audience to sell to - before they wake up.

Build B2B.
Build automations.
Find an existing application, slap ai onto it and make $.
